Discord:Tabs

Case Study - UX/UI Design

Introduction

Tabs is an organizational channel management tool for Discord.

My Role: UX Researcher, UI Designer

Team: James Halim (Individual Project)

Tools: Figma, Miro, After Affects

Timeline: June 2021 — August 2021

Problem Statement

Discord has had an increased in users from all different backgrounds. It's no longer just the voice communication platform for gamers. With that being said...

This prompted the following question:
‍How might we empower our diverse users to use Discord in the way they want, while making it simple, delightful and streamlined to use.

Phase 1: User Research

I kicked off the process with a comprehensive research phase - gathering quantitative data from user interviews and surveys

DESIGN GOALS:

Efficient: Tabs should seamlessly adapt to variable levels of information density across all channels

Accessible: Clean out the clutter, and help users easily complete their tasks.

Speed: Focusing on user jobs and workflows rather than silos of information.

Research Insights

USER INTERVIEWS INSIGHTS:

Too many chat channels can seem overbearing and makes the overall experience poor

Lack of organization system within channels

No clear way of accessing certain chat channels directly

Phase 2: Market Research

During user research phase, I noticed that users also used various other communication platforms. I chose the 4 most common applications and took note of the common features featured within each applications.

INSIGHTS:

After observing and using the other common communication apps, I realized that Discord was missing:

Bookmarking chat/voice channels

Creating and managing chat tabs

Built-in section for file sharing

Understanding how the other various apps function has helped me understand the core common features present in each app. From this I began my focus on a feature that would help Discord’s chat organization system.

Phase 3: Design Solution

My user-driven solution is a feature called "Tabs". Tabs allows users to interact with their Discord channels in a more personalized way. Users can manage and view many channels in an easier and more organized way.

In this design exploration process, I set out to create user-personas, information architecture, and various lo-fidelity designs.

User Personas: Focuses on a student that has experience in using Discord.

User Flow Chart: In the early stages of the design process, I set out to design a user flow chart that illustrates the user navigating from the onboarding stage to various pages within the app.


Sketches: Afterwards, I quickly sketched out some low-fidelity sketches. The goal in the sketching process was to come up with rapid designs and see which one would be most viable to implement in the final design.


Wireframes: After coming up with sketches, I created a series of mid-fidelity wireframes that can be quickly tested and refined over time. For the low fidelity prototypes, I explored a comprehensive set of user experience flows that would be split into three stages:

Phase 4: Testing & Iterations

Iterating and testing through unbiased and lean design

At this stage, I find it critical to involve the user within the design process. Based on various feedback from 6 other peers + mentor feedback, I continually iterated our designs over the span of 2 weeks. This resulted in 2 major improvements.

Lack of Visual Clarity: To address this, the “tabs” were were assigned its own unique visual identity in the form of a square shaped icon.

Accessibily Updates: To address this, I incorporated a bookmark feature for the “tabs”. Users are able to bookmark the “tabs” and can quick access it in the side menu.
