Petto

Case Study - UX/UI Design

Introduction

Petto is a mobile platform that enables users to find their perfect pet companion, keep track of the adoption process and interact with the pet community!

My Role: UX Researcher, UI Designer

Team: Janice Wang, James Halim

Tools: Figma, Miro, After Affects

Timeline: July 2021 — September 2021

Problem Statement

As a pet owner of a lovingly annoying cat, my partner and I noticed many of my friends and peers share the same struggles in adopting their lovely pets. This sparked the question...

This propmted the following question:
How might we create a seamless pet adoption process that helps users find the pets that fit their lifestyles?

Phase 1: User Research

Research Insights

Through a set of an empathy and infinity map, and user interviews, we found the following insights:

User Interview Insights: "Pet adoption process can be long and confusing"
"Too much time spent trying to find information through various sources"
"In most adoption websites and apps, the pet is displayed without any clear filters."

Phase 2: Design Solution

Our solution is to create an app that provides an accessible and streamlined pet adoption process users to enjoy. The app also provides all the necessary features conducted during the research phase. The features included are an easier adoption process, a social category where users can view posts regarding their pets, and keep track of their adoption process.

Based on our initial research findings, we came up with a set of 3 design pillars: Efficient, Social, and Manage.

Efficient focuses on streamlining the application adoption process for users. Social provides a space for users to interact with other pet owners for guidance. Manage is where users have the power to track their adoption process with full transparency.

Persona 1: Focuses on a user that has experience in pet adoption.

Persona 2: Focuses on a user that has little to no experience in pet adoption.

User Flow Chart: In the early stages of the design process, both my partner and I set out to design a user flow chart that illustrates the user navigating from the onboarding stage to various pages within the app.

Sketches: Afterwards, I quickly sketched out some low-fidelity sketches. The goal in the sketching process was to come up with rapid designs and see which one would be most viable to implement in the final design.

Lo-fi wireframes: After coming up with the low fidelity sketches, I created a series of low-fidelity wireframes that can be quickly tested and refined over time. For the low fidelity prototypes, I explored a comprehensive set of user experience flows that would be split into three stages:

P1. ONBOARDING PROCESS
2. PET ADOPTION PROCESS
3. COMMUNITY PAGE

Phase 3: Testing & Iterations

Iterating and testing through unbiased and lean design

At this stage, I find it critical to involve the user within the design process. Based on various feedback from 6 other peers + mentor feedback, I continually iterated our designs over the span of 2 weeks. This resulted in 3 major improvements.

An adoption tip was later added within the homepage to help users better understand how the adoption process works.

The updated login page now has a clear visual CTA (call to action) and follows the same visual pattern as seen in all buttons within the app.

The new icons have updated font colours that are more visible and passes the WCAG AA contrast standards.
